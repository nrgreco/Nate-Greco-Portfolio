Integrated Bar Guard Mounts

Overview

To protect my handlebars and controls while dirt biking, I generally use wraparound hand guards. These consist of an aluminum bar that connects to the end of the handlebar, a mount that secures to the inner portion of the handlebar, and a plastic shroud. However, the mounting solutions are often less than ideal — they can be finicky to fit, sometimes requiring shims or bending, since the guards are universal and cannot accommodate every handlebar shape.

On my bike with Renthal Twinwall handlebars, the common style bar mounts are incompatible due to the tapered design at the clamping area and interference from the factory crossbar mounts.

Design

To solve this, I designed a custom aluminum component that integrates the crossbar mount with the wraparound hand guard mount. Essentially, I created a stronger crossbar mount that copies the original geometry but includes a threaded hole extending outward to connect the hand guards.

The mount was designed as a two-piece construction so it can be removed without completely disassembly of the dirt bike controls. Since wraparound guards protect controls during crashes or tip-overs, the mount needed to be strong enough not to become the weak link.

Construction

I machined the mounts from a 1” thick 6061 aluminum plate. The two blanks were cut from the plate using a water jet, and then finished to the desired dimension on a milling machine. This produced a strong, precise part capable of withstanding the impacts expected in use.
